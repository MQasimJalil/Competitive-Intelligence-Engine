# Linear Competitor Profile

## At A Glance

Linear positions itself as the product-development operating system for teams and
AI agents, differentiating around opinionated workflows, speed, and agent-native
execution. [G001, G002]

## Offer And Pricing

- The product spans intake, product planning, execution with agents, code review,
  and progress monitoring. [G003]
- Packaging moves from Free to Basic at USD 10 per user/month and Business at USD
  16 per user/month, both with self-serve entry; Enterprise is custom and
  sales-led. [G004]
- Business concentrates several agent and intelligence features, while Enterprise
  adds identity, administration, security, migration, and account support. [G005, G006]

## Audience And Proof

- Linear says it serves more than 33,000 organizations, from startups to major
  enterprises, across SaaS, AI, fintech, consumer, hardware, and health. [G007, G008]
- Published customer evidence includes faster issue-resolution and agent-authored
  work metrics, though these should be treated as selected case-study outcomes.
  [G009]

## Commercial Interpretation

Linear is moving beyond issue tracking toward an agent-native product-development
system. The pricing structure supports broad adoption at the low end while placing
automation, intelligence, governance, and support into higher-value tiers.
[G002, G004, G005, G006]

## Recent Signals

Recent releases around team documents, structural diffs, project Slack channels,
and Code Intelligence reinforce the move toward shared context and human-agent
review workflows. [G010]
